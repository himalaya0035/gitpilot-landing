Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 15, 2026

Your privacy is important to us. It is GitPilot's policy to respect your privacy regarding any information we may collect from you across our application and website.

1. Information We Don't Collect

GitPilot is fundamentally designed as a local-first application. We do not collect, transmit, or store your source code, unencrypted repository contents, or private Git credentials on our servers. All codebase analysis and Git operations are performed purely on your local machine.

2. Data Collection & Usage

We may collect limited telemetry and usage data to improve the application. This may include:

  • Application crash reports and stack traces
  • Aggregated, anonymous feature usage statistics (e.g., number of visual workflows executed)
  • Basic hardware and OS information for compatibility optimization

You can opt-out of all telemetry data collection at any time via the Settings panel within the GitPilot application.

3. Third-Party Integrations

If you choose to use our remote syncing or cloud backup features, your workflow layouts (metadata only, no source code) may be saved securely using industry-standard encryption. We use secure cloud providers for this specific opted-in functionality.

4. Data Security

We take security seriously and implement reasonable security practices to protect your information. However, remember that no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure.

5. Contact Us

For more information about our privacy practices, if you have questions, or if you would like to make a complaint, please contact us by e-mail at privacy@gitpilot.dev.
